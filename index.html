Help Sharks

Sharks are among the most threatened marine species due to overfishing, habitat loss, and finning. Their populations have declined by over 70% in the last 50 years. Many species, such as the Great Hammerhead, Angelshark, and Oceanic Whitetip, are critically endangered. Protecting sharks is essential for healthy oceans.

Help Goats

Uncontrolled goat populations can lead to a range of environmental challenges, impacting vegetation, soil, biodiversity, and water quality. Understanding these effects is important for maintaining ecological balance in areas where goats are present. Uncontrolled goat populations can harm vegetation, soil, biodiversity, and water quality. Out of 576 goat breeds, 19 are extinct, 90 are at risk, and 310 have no conservation data. Conservation efforts are essential to protect rare and endangered goat breeds. Key Facts: Goats can adapt to a wide range of environments, from mountains to deserts. Mountain goats can climb extremely steep cliffs thanks to their split hooves and rough pads. Some goat breeds are critically endangered due to habitat loss and lack of conservation programs. Goats have been domesticated for over 10,000 years, making them one of the oldest livestock species. They play an important role in controlling vegetation when properly managed. Uncontrolled goat populations can damage soil, plants, and biodiversity. There are 576 recognized goat breeds worldwide, with many lacking conservation data. Goats are highly intelligent and social animals capable of forming strong bonds. They can survive in harsh climates due to their efficient digestive systems. Conservation efforts help protect rare goat breeds and maintain ecological balance.
